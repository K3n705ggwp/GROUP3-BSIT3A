Rationale

In recent years, the rapid expansion of e-commerce has transformed the way consumers purchase, offering unparalleled convenience via online platforms. Nonetheless, this convenience is frequently undermined by ongoing issues in the delivery process—especially missed or unsuccessful deliveries. These issues not only disrupt consumers but also impose higher operational costs and logistical inefficiencies on courier services. Moreover, customer worries about product authenticity, payment safety, and general trust in online platforms still influence buying habits.

Global technological innovations, like automated parcel lockers, have arisen to tackle these delivery issues, enhancing the effectiveness and safety of last-mile delivery. Although nations such as the UK and Poland have effectively implemented systems like InPost’s Automated Parcel Machines (APMs), localized solutions specifically designed for the Philippine context remain scarce. Initiatives such as the DALA Smart Lockbox have indicated potential in addressing this gap, highlighting the necessity for additional innovation and tailoring to meet local community dynamics, infrastructure, and consumer requirements.

This study addresses that requirement by suggesting the creation of an online parcel lockbox with a centralized management system, designed to enhance the unattended delivery process in the Philippines. The proposed solution is both practical and context-aware by incorporating features like secure PIN access, an automated cash dispenser for cash-on-delivery transactions, real-time verification using an ESP32 camera module, and a management rental system for property managers. It seeks to offer a more secure, accessible, and reliable delivery alternative for users—whether they are customers, couriers, or community administrators.

Furthermore, by evaluating the system based on the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)—specifically, its Perceived Usefulness (PU) and Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU)—the study ensures that both the technical performance and user satisfaction are critically assessed. This way, the study will check not only if the system works well technically, but also if it meets the needs and expectations of the users.

Ultimately, this project aims to address the typical delivery issues encountered by online consumers in the Philippines. The goal is to enhance the safety and convenience of deliveries, enabling more individuals to shop online confidently and fostering the ongoing expansion of e-commerce in the nation.
