OBJECTIVES

General Objective

This study aims to develop a Parcel Lockbox with Centralized Management System to provide a secure, automated, and efficient solution for unattended deliveries. It also seeks to address challenges related to missed deliveries and enhance last-mile delivery efficiency.

Specific Objectives

This study specifically aims to:

  1. Design and develop a smart parcel lockbox system that can: 
                 a. Detect parcels for their respective compartments in the lockbox;
           b. Dispense payment automatically for Cash-on-Delivery (COD) transactions and open automatically if digital payment is chosen;
           c. Capture and verify parcel deliveries using an ESP32 camera;
           d. Enable landlords, business managers, or school administrators to rent out compartments via a centralized management system;
           e. Allow the sharing of individual compartments among multiple users;
           f. Classify parcel box sizes into small, medium, and large categories;
           g. Generate unique PIN codes for user access instead of using hard-coded values;
           h. Determine and allocate vacant compartments automatically during user registration;
           i. Generate reports on delivery performance data from different shipping companies and online shopping platforms.
  2. Evaluate the system using the ISO 25010 software quality standards, specifically assessing: Functional Suitability, Performance Efficiency, Compatibility, Usability, Reliability, Security, Maintainability, and Portability.
